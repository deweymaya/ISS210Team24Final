“I walked to school and cried all the way. I just didn’t like it, you know, because they wasn’t teaching us nothing”. Darry Lester struggled to get the help he needed as he attended school. They didn’t give him the proper learning that he needed to be successful compared to students who were white and non-disabled. He was labeled mentally retarded along with other black students statewide based off an IQ test. These IQ tests determined who belong in special education. Parents complained of these tests being biased towards white culture. Darry's class structure was based on teaching the “educable mentally retarted" students personal hygiene and how to take care of themselves properly at home. It was as if this school didn’t believe these students could handle being taught real subjects in class. They already expected them to fail. Instead of these students getting help in class they would get sent to the principles office for them expressing their frustrations. The rate for black students to be expelled from school or sent home resulting in shorter school days is at a high. Black students who are also disabled are at an ever higher disadvantage. When schools don’t understand a student’s behavior, instead of assessing if they need to be in special education, they send them home from classes. They get punished for their behavior without any concern for their disability. It’s important to be aware of the issue of how school systems are treating black students with disability. Their education matters just as much as the next student.

What is ableism?

Ableism is a form of discrimination that is in favor of those who are able-bodied. Oftentimes, those who are disabled face prejudice because of being different. At its core, ableism is rooted in the belief that disabled people are in need of “fixing” and are inherently worse than others, and defined by their disability. There are many harmful stereotypes and generalizations that come with ableism along with sexism and racism. Everyday those who are disabled face microaggressions. Some ways to avoid ableism include: believing people when they disclose a disability, never assuming you know someone's needs, and educating yourself and others on ways they might be ableist without knowing.

Allegations of Student Discrimination

The Sacramento City Unified School District faces allegations of discrimination against students with disabilities. The disabled students attending this school have faced mistreatment by peers and faculty. Data was compiled and found that a majority of non-white disabled students attending this district were treated unequally. When it came to opportunities, discipline, segragationin classes, and achievement in classes, these students were faced with adversity.
